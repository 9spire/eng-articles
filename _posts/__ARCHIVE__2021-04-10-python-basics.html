Basic is Best

When it's been 5 years since using Python

I'd like to harken back to a Python project I worked on many years ago. Cast your mind on a much younger version of myself, idealistic and parallel parking a Ford F250 "Cowboy Cadillac" in downtown Portland to go to work at We Work (cringe here if you've seen the documentary).

I was working for Distinct Daily Media and every day was a new adventure in Python, Django, and the website I was building on top of all of that. Sadly, that website is no more. You may view the archeological version on the Wayback Machine: Distinct Daily. There was daily use of Python then, but that was five years ago, and although it's a programming language and follows all of the same concepts as any others, I wanted to brush up in the shortest time possible.

Course I took: Practical Python For Beginners
